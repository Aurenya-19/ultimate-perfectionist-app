High Weightage Topics (Focus 60% time):

  • Mechanics (30%): Kinematics, Laws of Motion, Work-Energy, Rotation, Gravitation
  • Electromagnetism (25%): Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Magnetism, EMI
  • Modern Physics (15%): Photoelectric Effect, Atoms, Nuclei
  • Thermodynamics (10%): Laws, Kinetic Theory, Heat Transfer
  • Optics + Waves (10%): Ray Optics, Wave Optics, SHM, Waves

Problem-Solving Approach:

  1. Draw diagram ALWAYS (even for simple problems)
  2. Write given data and required quantity
  3. Identify applicable laws/formulas
  4. Solve step-by-step with units
  5. Verify answer (dimensional analysis, limiting cases)

üéØ Mock Test Strategy

Mock Test Schedule:

  • Months 7-12: 1 JEE Main mock per week (Total: 24 mocks)
  • Months 13-16: 2 JEE Advanced mocks per week (Total: 32 mocks)
  • Last 2 months: 3 mocks per week (Total: 24 mocks)
  • Total Mocks: 80+ full-length tests

Mock Test Analysis (CRITICAL):

  1. Immediate Review (2 hours): Check all answers, understand mistakes
  2. Error Categorization:
    • Silly mistakes (calculation, reading error)
    • Concept gaps (didn't know the concept)
    • Application errors (knew concept, wrong application)
    • Time management (couldn't attempt)
  3. Action Plan: Note weak topics, plan revision
  4. Redo Wrong Questions: After 3 days, solve again

üß† Mental Preparation & Exam Strategy

Exam Day Strategy:

  1. First 15 minutes: Scan entire paper, mark easy/medium/hard
  2. Next 90 minutes: Solve all easy + medium questions (aim: 35-40 questions)
  3. Next 60 minutes: Attempt hard questions (aim: 10-12 questions)
  4. Last 15 minutes: Review, fill OMR carefully, verify

Question Selection:

  • Attempt 45-48 questions out of 54 (don't attempt all blindly)
  • Target 85-90% accuracy in attempted questions
  • Skip if you're not 70% confident
  • Negative marking is your enemy: -2 for wrong, +4 for correct
